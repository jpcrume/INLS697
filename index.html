Top 5 Financial Scams Targeting Older Adults

​The National Council on Aging (NCOA) identifies five prevalent financial scams targeting older adults. These include sweepstakes and lottery scams, where victims are told they've won a prize but must pay fees to claim it; robocalls and phone scams, such as the "Can you hear me?" tactic used to record a voice signature for unauthorized charges; business imposter scams, where fraudsters pose as legitimate companies to solicit payments or personal information; government impersonation scams, involving callers pretending to be from agencies like the IRS to demand immediate payment; and tech support scams, where individuals are deceived into believing their computers are compromised, leading them to grant remote access or pay for unnecessary services. The NCOA emphasizes the importance of awareness and caution to protect against these deceptive practices.​

ChatGPT’s new image generator is really good at faking receipts

​In a recent article by Charles Rollet for TechCrunch, OpenAI's latest image generator, integrated into ChatGPT's 4o model, has demonstrated a remarkable ability to create realistic fake receipts. This advancement has sparked concerns about its potential misuse in fraudulent activities, such as submitting fabricated expenses for reimbursement. Users have shared examples on social media of AI-generated receipts that closely mimic authentic ones, complete with details like food stains and crumpled textures. Although some generated receipts contain minor inaccuracies, such as incorrect formatting or calculation errors, these can be easily corrected with basic editing tools. OpenAI has responded by stating that all images produced by the generator include metadata indicating their origin and that the company actively monitors and addresses violations of its usage policies. While the tool offers creative applications, its capacity to produce convincing counterfeit documents raises significant ethical and security concerns.​

March 31, 2025

Deepfake videos are getting shockingly good

​A recent TechCrunch article highlights the advancements in deepfake technology, focusing on ByteDance's new AI system, OmniHuman-1. This tool can generate highly realistic videos from just a single image and audio clip, producing content that closely mimics real-life footage. While the technology showcases impressive capabilities, it raises concerns about potential misuse, including political misinformation and financial fraud. The article underscores the need for regulatory measures and public awareness to address the ethical implications of such powerful AI tools.​
