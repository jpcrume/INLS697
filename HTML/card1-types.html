Identifying Types of Misinformation

Misinformation and disinformation are both forms of false information that can mislead or manipulate public perception. While misinformation refers to false or inaccurate information spread unintentionally, disinformation involves the deliberate creation and distribution of misleading or false content to deceive or manipulate audiences. Underneath the umbrella term of ‘misinformation’ lies many sub-types with the types most prevalent being:

  1. Fabricated Content
  2. Manipulated Content
  3. Imposter Content
  4. Misleading Content
  5. False Context
  6. Satire and Parody
  7. False Connections
  8. Sponsored Content
  9. Propaganda
  10. Error
